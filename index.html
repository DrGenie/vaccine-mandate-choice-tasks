To mandate or not to mandate?

Public preferences for vaccine mandates in Australia and international comparators

Brief Background: Government COVID‑19 vaccine mandates have included employment and public space mandates. This study aims to quantify public preferences and trade‑offs among key features of future vaccine mandates.

Attribute Scorecard

Allocate a total of 100 points among the following attributes to indicate their importance. Higher scores mean the attribute matters more when choosing a preferred vaccine mandate. If you do not consider an attribute relevant, please assign it 0 points so that the total equals 100.

Attribute Levels, Description & Rationale Points
Scope of Mandate ℹ️ High‑risk occupations only: Required for individuals in high‑risk roles.
All occupations and public spaces: Required for everyone working and for accessing public venues.
Rationale: Balances targeted intervention with universal coverage.
Mandate Administration Level ℹ️ Local Mandate: Enforced by local authorities.
National Mandate: Uniform enforcement by central government.
Private Mandate: Imposed by private organizations.
Rationale: Reflects differences in enforcement, legitimacy, and reach.
Infection Threshold for Mandate Activation ℹ️ 50 cases per 100k with 10% weekly increase: Early trigger.
100 cases per 100k with 15% weekly increase: Moderate trigger.
200 cases per 100k with 20% weekly increase: Late trigger under a severe outbreak.
Rationale: A moderate threshold is generally preferred.
Vaccine Coverage Requirement for Lifting Mandates ℹ️ Low (50%): Lifting mandates with 50% coverage.
Moderate (70%): A balanced threshold ensuring substantial protection.
High (90%): Requires near‑universal vaccination.
Rationale: Moderate coverage (70%) is realistic and effective.
Incentives for Vaccination ℹ️ No incentives: No additional rewards.
Paid time off for vaccination (1–3 days): Compensates for lost work time.
Government subsidies/discounts on services: Provides direct financial incentive.
Rationale: Incentives lower the personal cost and encourage uptake.
Exemption Policy ℹ️ Medical exemptions only: Only those with verified health risks can opt out.
Medical and religious exemptions: Opt-out permitted for health and religious reasons.
Medical, religious and broad personal belief exemptions: Allows a wide range of opt‑outs.
Rationale: Balances fairness with maximizing vaccination uptake.
Opportunity Cost of Compliance ℹ️ Low (A$5): Minimal inconvenience.
Moderate (A$20): Typical cost in urban settings.
High (A$50): Significant cost (e.g., long travel or wage loss).
Rationale: Higher costs require additional incentives.

Scenario Description

Imagine a future where a new pandemic has emerged, caused by a novel virus different from COVID‑19 but with similar global impact. Safe and effective vaccines have been developed and are available to everyone. Governments worldwide are considering different ways to control the spread of this new disease – including making vaccines mandatory. In this survey, you will compare two different hypothetical vaccine mandate policies and decide which one you prefer.

Instructions

  • You will be presented with 9 choice tasks – one per page.
  • Each task compares two vaccine mandate options (A and B) with different attribute levels.
  • Please read each task carefully and select your preferred option.
  • If given the option, indicate if your choice would remain the same if you could opt out.
  • All responses are mandatory.
