To mandate or not to mandate? Vaccine Mandate Survey (For Research Team Only!)

Understanding Vaccine Mandate Features

Welcome! In this survey, we’re looking at how vaccination policies might be structured if a new pandemic (different from COVID‑19) were to emerge, and safe, effective vaccines were available.

You’ll first see tutorials on six key features of a potential vaccine mandate: scope, threshold, coverage, incentives, exemptions, and cost. Hover or tap on the small icons (like ⚠️, 🌐, 💰) for more detailed explanations of each level.

Afterwards, you’ll pick the block you’ve been assigned (1–4). Each block has 9 scenarios. In each scenario, you’ll compare two possible mandates (Vaccine Mandate A and Vaccine Mandate B) and decide which you prefer, then whether you’d opt out if possible. We’ll attempt to email your final answers. If that fails, you can download them as CSV. Thanks for taking part!

Scope of mandate: Who Might Be Covered by a Mandate?

High‑risk occupations only: ⚠️ This targets those who are most likely to contract or spread the virus.

All occupations and public spaces: 🌐 Covers everyone 18+ in public or work environments, aiming for broad protection.

Incentives for Vaccination: Possible Incentives

No incentives: 🚫 Individuals who vaccinate do so without extra perks.

Paid time off (1–3 days): 🕒 Minimises financial/scheduling barriers.

A 10% discount on government services: 💸 Tangible financial benefit.

Exemption policy: Who Can Opt Out?

Medical exemptions only: 🩺 Very narrow, requiring verified medical evidence.

Medical and religious exemptions: 🩺🙏 Balances health and religious considerations.

Medical, religious, and broad personal belief: 🩺🙏💡 Most flexible approach.

Opportunity cost: Cost or Burden of Getting Vaccinated

No opportunity cost (AUD0): 💰 x 0 Zero financial burden.

Low (AUD5): 💰 A small but noticeable cost.

Moderate (AUD20): 💰💰 Could deter those with limited resources.

High (AUD50): 💰💰💰 Major deterrent for cost-conscious individuals.
