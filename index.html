To mandate or not to mandate? Vaccine Mandate Survey

Understanding Vaccine Mandate Characteristics

This survey explores how various features of a vaccine mandate influence your decision. First, you will review tutorials describing six key attributes and their levels. Next, you will see the scenario description and an example choice task. Finally, you will complete nine choice tasks.

Tutorial: Scope of Mandate

What it means: Who is required to be vaccinated.

High‑risk occupations only: ⚠️ Only essential workers are mandated.
Only those in critical sectors such as hospitals or emergency services are required.

All occupations and public spaces: 🌐 Everyone is mandated.
Every person, whether working or visiting public areas, must be vaccinated.

Tutorial: Infection Threshold for Mandate Activation

What it means: The infection level that activates the mandate.

50 cases per 100k with 10% weekly increase: 🟢 Mandate starts at a low level.
This triggers the mandate when infection levels are still low.

100 cases per 100k with 15% weekly increase: 🟠 Mandate begins at a moderate level.
This is a balanced trigger point for starting the mandate.

200 cases per 100k with 20% weekly increase: 🔴 Mandate activates late.
The mandate starts only when infections become very high.

Tutorial: Vaccine Coverage Requirement for Lifting Mandates

What it means: The vaccination rate needed to end the mandate.

Low (50%): Mandate ends at 50% coverage.
The restrictions end once half the population is vaccinated.

Moderate (70%): Mandate ends at 70% coverage.
The mandate is lifted once a substantial majority is vaccinated.

High (90%): Mandate ends at 90% coverage.
Restrictions are removed only when nearly everyone is vaccinated.

Tutorial: Incentives for Vaccination

What it means: The rewards offered to encourage vaccination.

No incentives: 🚫 No extra benefits are provided.
This option provides no additional motivation.

Paid time off (1–3 days): 🕒 Offers extra days off.
This compensates for the time you spend getting vaccinated.

Government subsidies/discounts: 💸 Offers financial rewards.
This option gives you money or discounts to offset costs.

Tutorial: Exemption Policy

What it means: Who is allowed to opt out of the mandate.

Medical exemptions only: 🩺 Only people with proven health issues may opt out.
This limits opt-outs to those with genuine medical needs.

Medical and religious exemptions: 🩺🙏 Permits opt-outs for health or religious reasons.
This option allows for broader exceptions.

Medical, religious and broad personal belief exemptions: 🩺🙏💡 Permits opt-outs for many reasons.
This option offers the most flexibility, which may reduce overall vaccination rates.

Tutorial: Opportunity Cost of Compliance

Scenario Description

Imagine a future where a new pandemic has emerged—caused by a novel virus different from COVID‑19 but with similar global impact. Safe and effective vaccines are available to everyone. Governments worldwide are considering policies—including mandatory vaccination—to control the spread. Although all vaccines are effective, the details of the mandate can vary. In each choice task, you will compare two hypothetical vaccine mandate policies and choose the one you prefer.
