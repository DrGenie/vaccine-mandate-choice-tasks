Understanding Vaccine Mandate Features

Welcome! In this survey, we’re examining how vaccination policies might be designed if a new pandemic (different from COVID‑19) were to emerge, and safe, effective vaccines were available.

You’ll first see tutorials covering six key features of a potential vaccine mandate: scope, threshold, coverage, incentives, exemptions, and cost. Hover or tap on icons (like ⚠️, 🌐, 💰) for detailed explanations.

Afterwards, you’ll select which block of scenarios (1–4) you’re assigned. Each block has 9 scenarios. In each scenario, you’ll compare Vaccine Mandate A to Vaccine Mandate B and choose which you prefer, then decide if you’d opt out entirely if possible. We’ll attempt to email your results; if that fails, you can download them as CSV. Thank you for participating!

Scope of mandate: Who Might Be Covered by a Mandate?

High‑risk occupations only: ⚠️ Targets those most likely to catch or spread infection.

All occupations and public spaces: 🌐 Aims for broad coverage of the entire population.

Infection threshold: When a Mandate Might Start

50 cases per 100k with 10% weekly increase 🟢
Triggers a mandate while overall cases remain relatively low, aiming to contain spread quickly.

100 cases per 100k with 15% weekly increase 🟠
A middle ground, stepping in when the outbreak is noticeable but not severe.

200 cases per 100k with 20% weekly increase 🔴
Allows more freedom initially but steps in only when transmission is already high.

Coverage requirement: When a Mandate Might End

50% coverage
Mandate lifts relatively quickly but leaves a large unvaccinated portion.

70% coverage
A commonly cited threshold for herd immunity in some diseases, though exact numbers can vary.

90% coverage
An ambitious target, ending only with near-universal vaccination.

Incentives for Vaccination: Possible Incentives

No incentives 🚫
Individuals vaccinate without extra perks or compensation.

Paid time off (1–3 days) 🕒
Offsets lost wages or scheduling issues, lowering financial barriers.

A 10% discount on government services 💸
Provides tangible cost savings for compliance.

Exemption policy: Who Can Opt Out?

Medical exemptions only 🩺
Highly restrictive, requiring physician documentation.

Medical and religious exemptions 🩺🙏
Broader than medical-only, allowing religion-based opt-outs.

Medical, religious, and broad personal belief 🩺🙏💡
Most flexible, enabling more people to decline vaccination.

Opportunity cost: Cost or Burden of Getting Vaccinated

No opportunity cost (AUD0) 💰 x 0
Truly effortless, removing financial barriers.

Low (AUD5) 💰
Minor out-of-pocket cost, easily affordable for most.

Moderate (AUD20) 💰💰
More significant burden, potentially deterring those on tight budgets.

High (AUD50) 💰💰💰
Major deterrent for many, significantly raising barriers.
