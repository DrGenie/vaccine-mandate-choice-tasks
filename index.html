To mandate or not to mandate? Vaccine Mandate Survey (For Research Team Only!)

Understanding Vaccine Mandate Features

Welcome! In this survey, we’re exploring how vaccination policies might be structured if a new pandemic (different from COVID‑19) emerged, with safe and effective vaccines available.

You’ll first see tutorials on six key attributes of a possible vaccine mandate, such as who’s covered, when it begins/ends, incentives, exemptions, and personal costs. Tip: Hover (or tap) on icons (e.g., ⚠️, 🌐) to see short explanations for each attribute level.

Afterwards, you’ll pick which block you’re assigned to (1–4). Each block has 9 scenarios. In each scenario, compare Vaccine Mandate A vs. Vaccine Mandate B. Choose the option you prefer, then say if you’d choose “no mandate” if it were available. Finally, the system attempts to email your responses to the research team. If that fails, you can download them as a CSV file. Thank you for taking part!

Scope of mandate: Who Might Be Covered by a Mandate?

High‑risk occupations only: ⚠️ Targets those most likely to catch or spread infection.

All occupations and public spaces: 🌐 Universally applies to the entire adult population.

Infection threshold: When a Mandate Might Start

50 cases per 100k with 10% weekly increase: 🟢 Lower threshold—stricter in intervening sooner.

100 cases per 100k with 15% weekly increase: 🟠 Medium threshold—some risk allowed before acting.

200 cases per 100k with 20% weekly increase: 🔴 Late trigger—higher outbreak risk.

Coverage requirement: When a Mandate Might End

50% coverage: Ends relatively early, leaving more room for virus spread among the unvaccinated.

70% coverage: Lifts once a sizable majority is vaccinated.

90% coverage: Strict approach—ends only with near-universal coverage.

Incentives for Vaccination: Possible Incentives

No incentives: 🚫 Individuals receive no additional support.

Paid time off (1–3 days): 🕒 Eases financial burdens of scheduling.

A 10% discount on government services: 💸 Tangible monetary benefit.

Exemption policy: Who Can Opt Out?

Medical exemptions only: 🩺 Narrow approach.

Medical and religious exemptions: 🩺🙏 Broader approach.

Medical, religious, and broad personal belief: 🩺🙏💡 Most flexible.

Opportunity cost: Cost or Burden of Getting Vaccinated

No opportunity cost (AUD0): 💰 x 0 Zero burden.

Low (AUD5): 💰 Minor out-of-pocket cost.

Moderate (AUD20): 💰💰 Could deter some on tight budgets.

High (AUD50): 💰💰💰 Major deterrent.

The Scenario

Next, you’ll be shown 9 vaccine mandate scenarios from your assigned block (1 to 4). Each scenario compares Vaccine Mandate A and Vaccine Mandate B, differing by scope, threshold, coverage, incentives, exemptions, and cost.

Decide which mandate you prefer, then let us know if you’d keep that preference if you could opt out entirely. If email submission fails, a CSV download will be available for you or the research team to retrieve your responses. Let’s proceed!
