To mandate or not to mandate? Vaccine Mandate Survey

Understanding Vaccine Mandate Characteristics

In this survey, we want to learn how different features of a vaccine mandate affect your decision. You will first review a series of tutorial slides that describe six key attributes and their levels. Then you will see the full scenario description, an example choice task, and finally complete nine choice tasks.

Scope of Mandate

What it means: This attribute defines who is required to be vaccinated.

High‑risk occupations only: ⚠️
(Only essential workers, such as nurses and firefighters, must be vaccinated.)

All occupations and public spaces: 🌐
(Every person, regardless of job or location, must be vaccinated.)

Infection Threshold for Mandate Activation

What it means: This attribute sets the level of infections needed to activate the mandate.

50 cases per 100k with 10% weekly increase: 🟢
(The mandate starts when infections are low—helping to prevent a rapid outbreak.)

100 cases per 100k with 15% weekly increase: 🟠
(A balanced level, intervening when the outbreak is noticeable but not extreme.)

200 cases per 100k with 20% weekly increase: 🔴
(The mandate is delayed until the outbreak is very serious.)

Incentives for Vaccination

What it means: This attribute describes any rewards offered to encourage vaccination.

No incentives: 🚫
(There are no extra benefits offered.)

Paid time off (1–3 days): 🕒
(You receive extra days off to make up for the time spent getting vaccinated.)

Government subsidies/discounts: 💸
(You get money or discounts that help offset the cost of getting vaccinated.)

Exemption Policy

What it means: This attribute outlines who is permitted to opt out of the mandate.

Medical exemptions only: 🩺
(Only people with proven medical issues are allowed to skip vaccination.)

Medical and religious exemptions: 🩺🙏
(People can opt out if they have medical problems or sincere religious objections.)

Medical, religious and broad personal belief exemptions: 🩺🙏💡
(A wide range of reasons are accepted, so more people may choose not to get vaccinated.)

Scenario Description

Imagine a future where a new pandemic has emerged—caused by a novel virus different from COVID‑19 but with a similar global impact. Safe and effective vaccines are available to everyone. Governments around the world are considering different policies—including mandatory vaccination—to control the spread of the disease. Although all vaccines are safe and effective, the details of how a mandate is implemented can vary. In each choice task, you will compare two hypothetical vaccine mandate policies and decide which one you prefer.
