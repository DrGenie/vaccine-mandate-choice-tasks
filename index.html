To mandate or not to mandate? Vaccine Mandate Survey (For Research Team Only!)

Understanding Vaccine Mandate Features

Welcome! In this survey, we explore how a hypothetical vaccine mandate might be designed if a new pandemic emerged (different from COVID‑19), with safe and effective vaccines available.

You’ll first see tutorials explaining key attributes of a possible vaccine mandate, like who is covered, when it begins or ends, whether incentives are offered, what exemptions might apply, and how much personal cost might be involved.

Tip: Many attributes have small icons (⚠️, 🌐, 🩺, etc.). Hover or tap on them to see a brief tooltip explaining that level in plain words.

Afterwards, you’ll be assigned to one of 4 blocks (each block has 9 scenarios). In each scenario, you’ll compare Vaccine Mandate A to Vaccine Mandate B. Pick the one you prefer, then tell us if you’d opt out altogether if that were allowed. When done, we’ll attempt to email your responses to the research team; if it fails, a CSV download will be available. Thanks for participating!

Scope of mandate: Who Might Be Covered by a Mandate?

High‑risk occupations only: ⚠️ Limits mandates to those with greater infection or transmission risk.

All occupations and public spaces: 🌐 Everyone must vaccinate, regardless of job role or location.

Infection threshold: When a Mandate Might Start

50 cases per 100k with 10% weekly increase: 🟢 Lower threshold, intervening at a relatively small outbreak size.

100 cases per 100k with 15% weekly increase: 🟠 Balanced approach, not too early or too late.

200 cases per 100k with 20% weekly increase: 🔴 Higher threshold, riskier in terms of outbreak control.

Coverage requirement: When a Mandate Might End

50% coverage: Ends relatively early with many still unvaccinated.

70% coverage: A common herd immunity threshold, though exact figures can vary.

90% coverage: Strict requirement, ending only with very high coverage.

Incentives for Vaccination: Possible Incentives

No incentives: 🚫 Individuals see no extra reward for vaccinating.

Paid time off (1–3 days): 🕒 Eases the burden of time away from work.

A 10% discount on government services: 💸 Tangible monetary benefit.

Exemption policy: Who Can Opt Out?

Medical exemptions only: 🩺 Very narrow policy.

Medical and religious exemptions: 🩺🙏 Broader grounds for opting out.

Medical, religious, and broad personal belief: 🩺🙏💡 Most flexible, allowing more people to skip vaccination.

Opportunity cost: Cost or Burden of Getting Vaccinated

No opportunity cost (AUD0): 🪙 x 0 Zero burden.

Low (AUD5): 🪙 Minor out-of-pocket cost.

Moderate (AUD20): 🪙🪙 A significant but not extreme barrier.

High (AUD50): 🪙🪙🪙 Major burden.
